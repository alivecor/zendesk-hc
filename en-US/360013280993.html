What readings can be generated by the Kardia Instant Analysis?

The following results can occur with the Kardia App Instant Analysis:
NOTE: These are only proven using a "LEAD I" EKG. For any other type of EKG reading, customer should not rely on the Instant Analysis reading

  • NORMAL
    • The heart rate is within 50-100 beats per minute
    • The app has not detected any indicators for Possible Atrial Fibrillation
    • The app determined the reading to be clean and fairly free from outside interference
  • POSSIBLE ATRIAL FIBRILLATION
    • The app has detected signs that the user may be experiencing an AFIB episode
    • There is a margin of error as the app has been proven to be accurate to 97% sensitivity and 98% specificity.
      • Sensitivity - Ability to identify those with a disease/condition
      • Specificity - Ability to identify those that specifically do not have a disease/condition
    • The app determined the reading to be clean and fairly free from outside interference
  • UNCLASSIFIED
    • The app cannot make a conclusive determination that a customer is in either Normal Sinus Rhythm (the Normal reading) or a possible AFib episode (the Possible Atrial Fibrillation reading)
    • This reading does not necessarily mean that the customer has a health issue at that time, nor does it mean there may not be something else occurring.  It simply means that the device cannot make a determination based on what the app is designed to detect.
    • The app determined the reading to be clean and fairly free from outside interference

  • UNREADABLE
    • The app has determined there to be too much noise/interference detected captured by the microphone to be order to take a proper reading.

  • NO ANALYSIS
    • EKG readings require a minimum of 30 seconds in order to make an Instant Analysis.  Any reading between 10 and 29 seconds will prompt the customer whether they want to re-record the test, or to save it.  If they save it, it will save as No Analysis.
    • It may also be possible to get this notice due to noise or interference.  But more commonly that would appear as Unreadable.
