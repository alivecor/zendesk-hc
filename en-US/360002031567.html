What is a Potential Adverse Event and how does Zendesk need to be coded if we receive a call to that effect?

A "Potential Adverse Event", as it relates to Customer Care, is any customer contact where they claim to have been injured while using a Kardia product.

A common example of a Potential Adverse Event is a customer using the KardiaBand who says they developed a rash while using our product.

There are procedures that Alivecor must follow when dealing with a "P.A.E." so it is important that these are appropriately coded and escalated.  To that end these are the steps that must be followed:

  1. The correct support code to be used is: Complaint/EKG Hardware Issue/Other.

  2. The "Potential Advise Event (PAE) Checkbox MUST be checked.

  3. The ticket must then be escalated to Level 2 Support.

It is also recommended that in your subject line that you put a reference to a Potential Adverse Event.
